## What we are selling (the frame every criterion serves)

Bonsai Instant Citation — a **browser extension** that builds MLA / APA / Chicago / IEEE
citations in a couple of clicks, on the page the student is reading, with the in-text form
beside it. The three facts that decide who is a good fit:

1. **Zero data collection.** No accounts, no network requests, on-device only (manifest v3,
   minimal permissions — audited CONSISTENT with the zero-collection posture). To a school
   this means **nothing to FERPA-review** — the single biggest friction-remover in ed-tech
   sales.
2. **Force-installs through Google Admin in an afternoon.** One IT push covers every student.
   So the real buyer is **whoever controls the Google Workspace / Chromebook fleet.**
3. **Flat pricing by enrollment.** No per-seat math, no quote maze — an easy yes for a
   network with central procurement.

## The buying unit (who actually says yes)

A pitch only converts when three roles align. Score a target on how easily you can reach all three:

- **CHAMPION** — feels the citation pain daily: a **librarian**, **English/writing department
  head**, **research-skills or IB/AP coordinator**, or **writing-center director**.
- **GATEKEEPER** — clears privacy/curriculum: the person who would normally run a FERPA review.
  Bonsai's zero-collection posture is aimed squarely at making this a non-event.
- **INSTALLER** — the **Google Workspace / Chromebook admin** who runs the force-install.

A target where one person is two of these roles (e.g. a charter network's academic director who
also owns central IT) is a **high-fit target** — fewer handoffs, faster pilot.

## Scoring rubric (0–3 each; sum is the ICP score, max 15)

| Dimension | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Fleet control** | BYOD, no central management | mixed | mostly managed Chromebooks | Google Admin-managed 1:1 fleet |
| **Citation load** | little formal writing | some | research-writing in the curriculum | IB/AP/dual-enrollment, heavy citation |
| **Reach to a champion** | cold, no warm path | title known | named person reachable | warm intro available |
| **Privacy posture fit** | strict, slow review board | standard | privacy-conscious, values on-device | actively wary of data-collecting tools |
| **Scale per yes** | single classroom | one school | small district | multi-campus network / district |

- **12–15 · PRIORITY** — pitch first; strong fit on fleet + scale + reachability.
- **8–11 · QUALIFIED** — worth a personalized email; note the weakest dimension and address it.
- **≤7 · PARK** — revisit later or when a warm path appears. Do not spend cold-outreach effort.

## Priority archetypes (proven by the outreach drafts on file)

1. **School librarian / media specialist** — owns citation instruction; privacy is their language.
2. **English / writing department head** — feels the mangled Works Cited pages directly.
3. **Charter-network academic director (central IT)** — one force-install covers every campus;
   flat enrollment pricing fits central procurement. **Highest fit** when academic + IT authority sit together.
4. **Writing-center director (dual-enrollment / early-college HS)** — heavy citation load, motivated champion.
5. **AP / IB coordinator** — research papers are a graded requirement; citation correctness is a standing need.

## Disqualifiers (do not spend outreach here)

- No managed Chrome/Google fleet (can't force-install → no clean pilot path).
- A hard "no third-party extensions" district policy with no exception process.
- Higher-ed procurement mazes requiring an RFP for a free 60-day pilot (park until there's a champion).
- Anyone requiring student PII to evaluate — the product collects none; that ask is a mismatch.
